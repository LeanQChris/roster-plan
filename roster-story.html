Beyond MVP — The Road Ahead

MVP gives GreenLeaf Cafe working scheduling and clock-in. Here's how Roster grows across 4 post-MVP phases — each unlocking new capabilities for Sarah, James, Maya, and Alex.

11
Phase A — Before the Shift

Self-scheduling, time off & smarter org data

In Phase A, the biggest shift is self-scheduling. Instead of James assigning every shift, Maya can now browse open shifts and pick them up herself. James sees requests come in and approves or denies them with a single tap. If Maya tries to claim a shift that overlaps with an existing assignment or approved time-off, conflict detection warns her immediately.

Maya can also request time off — James reviews the request alongside a coverage impact preview showing how the absence would affect the schedule. Shift swaps between teammates follow the same approval pattern.

Sarah fills out her org with positions (line cook, sous chef, dishwasher with pay rates), skills (CPR certified, forklift license), and locations (downtown cafe, east side commissary). She creates a company holiday calendar so the system knows which days are paid. A feature flags page lets her toggle which capabilities are active per location. Maya can set her timezone in her profile so her schedule always displays in her local time.

Notification upgrades arrive: email reminders before a shift, a weekly digest of upcoming shifts, and — for the first time — calendar export so Maya can sync her schedule to Google Calendar or Apple Calendar via iCal or webcal subscription. Password reset is now self-service — Maya can reset her own password if she forgets it. Rate limiting (60 req/min for reads, 10 for writes) protects the API from abuse.

Phase B — During the Shift

Break tracking, live attendance & integrations

Maya clocks in for her shift. Now, instead of a single clock-in/out, the system prompts her to log meal and rest breaks — recording each break start and end time automatically. If she forgets, the system flags a compliance concern.

James opens a Live Attendance board that updates in real time: who's clocked in on time, who's late, who's on break, who hasn't shown up. He can see at a glance that Bob is 18 minutes late and Carol is on her meal break.

Sarah sets up integrations — a Slack connection posts shift reminders to #kitchen-alerts automatically. The Microsoft Teams connector isn't configured yet but the UI is ready.

13
Phase C — After the Shift

Reports, compliance & audit UI

Now that Roster has been running for months, James wants to see patterns. He opens the Attendance Report — scheduled vs actual hours for July. Bob has been late twice. Alice is spotless. A CSV export button lets him pull the data for payroll.

The Overtime Report shows who's exceeding 40 hours per week — Alice at 44h, Carol at 42h. The Coverage Report uses a visual heatmap to highlight gaps at a glance: Wednesday July 16 shows 50% fill rate in red. The Compliance Report surfaces violations: missed meal breaks, late clock-ins, no-shows.

Sarah opens the Company Audit Log — a filterable chronological view of every action. She configures Compliance Settings (overtime thresholds, minimum rest periods, data retention). The Data Management page gives her GDPR erasure: she selects a person and permanently anonymizes their personal data while preserving clock entries.

14
Phase D — Scale & Enterprise

Data residency, SSO, billing & public API

GreenLeaf Cafe has grown to 20 locations across 3 countries. Sarah's data needs to stay in Australia. Alex configures data residency routing — GreenLeaf's data now lives on an AU cluster, while a US-based client routes to us-east.

Sarah's parent company requires SSO/OIDC — employees log in with their corporate Google Workspace accounts instead of Roster passwords. Alex enables it from the admin panel with a few clicks.

Alex sets up Billing — GreenLeaf is on the Pro plan ($99/mo), 50 active employees, annual discount applied. A team of developers wants to build a custom payroll integration, so Sarah generates a Public API key scoped to read-only shift data.
